Teaching

Courses emphasize conceptual clarity, rigorous methods, and practical analytic skills that students can apply in research and policy settings.

University of Texas at Dallas

  • Applied Regression (Graduate): A hands-on introduction to linear and generalized linear models for political and social scientists. Covers OLS, diagnostics, interactions, logistic and count models, and the interpretation of results in R.
  • Data and Text Analysis (Graduate): An applied course on working with large and unstructured data. Topics include data wrangling, visualization, supervised and unsupervised text classification, topic models, and word embeddings for social science research.
  • Logic, Methodology, and Scope of Political Science (PSCI 6350, Graduate): A core doctoral seminar on research design, causal inference, and the major modes of analysis used in political science.
  • International Conflict (Undergraduate): Explores the causes, dynamics, and consequences of interstate and intrastate conflict, drawing on bargaining theory, quantitative evidence, and historical cases.
  • Introduction to International Relations (Undergraduate): A survey of the major theories, actors, and issues in world politics, including security, cooperation, and political economy.

Penn State — Graduate

  • PLSC 502 — Quantitative Political Analysis: Second-semester doctoral methods course covering the linear model, inference, and extensions for political science research.
  • PLSC — Machine Learning for Political Science: Graduate introduction to supervised and unsupervised learning for political and social data, emphasizing prediction, regularization, trees, and applied workflows in R and Python.
  • SoDA 501 — Approaches to Social Data Analytics: Interdisciplinary graduate course on collecting, managing, and analyzing social data, including APIs, text, networks, and reproducible research practices.

Penn State — Undergraduate (SoDA)

  • Data Visualization: Principles and practice of visual communication with data. Students build charts, maps, and interactive graphics using modern tools and learn to design visualizations for research and policy audiences.
