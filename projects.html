Projects

Ongoing projects center on methodological innovation and practical research impact, combining computational tools with substantive political science applications.

Computational Methods for Political Science

Design and validation of end-to-end pipelines for text, event, and relational data. This project develops reusable frameworks for measurement, model comparison, and transparent reporting in high-dimensional settings.

  • Core methods: LLM-assisted coding, NLP, graph learning, uncertainty quantification.
  • Current output: reproducible templates for collaborative political data analysis.

Conflict and International Security Collaboration

Multi-institution collaboration studying escalation pathways, deterrence dynamics, and conflict signaling under conditions of incomplete information. Emphasis is on integrating theory and empirical models.

  • Data: event streams, strategic interactions, policy communication records.
  • Research goal: improve forecasting and explanation of conflict behavior.

American Political Media

Analysis of partisan framing, agenda-setting, and attention cycles across traditional and digital media. The project examines how narrative structure influences public interpretation and elite behavior.

  • Approach: topic modeling, semantic similarity, and networked discourse mapping.
  • Substantive focus: polarization, credibility, and information asymmetries.

Supply Chain Disruptions

Interdisciplinary work on how geopolitical shocks and conflict events propagate through supply networks. The project links political risk to bottlenecks, resilience strategies, and policy response.

  • Methods: network centrality diagnostics, stress testing, and comparative case analysis.
  • Contribution: clearer models of exposure, adaptation, and downstream effects.

Aid Distribution

Research on allocation rules, delivery constraints, and implementation tradeoffs in complex environments. Special focus is given to targeting quality, local governance, and strategic behavior by intermediaries.

  • Evidence base: geospatial indicators, administrative records, and text reports.
  • Objective: identify where distribution systems perform well or fail.

Human Rights Proliferation

Investigation of how rights norms travel across institutions, legal frameworks, and policy discourse. The project tracks both diffusion mechanisms and points of resistance over time.

  • Analytic lens: institutional adoption, framing variation, and norm contestation.
  • Expected outputs: comparative studies and data resources for future scholarship.
